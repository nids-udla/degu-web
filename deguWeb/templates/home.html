Human, Degu, Naked Mole Rat and House Mouse.

Welcome to the Comparative Genomics and Orthologies Groups Tool (CGO) to analise the genomes of Octodon degus , Homo sapiens , Heterocephalus glaber and Mus musculus .

Long-lived rodents can help us understand common evolutionary paths to aging. Several studies have shown that some of the key marks of aging, including neuropathological and cognitive exist not only in man but in some other species. We performed a comparative genomic study of two long-lived rodents the aging-prone Octodon degus (degu) and the virtually aging-free Heterocephalus glaber (naked mole rat, NMR) with the aim to discover genetic factors responsible for their significant differences in relation to aging. We included mus musculus and homo sapiens in the comparative genomic studies. As degus, NMR and mice are in the family Rodentia, these studies are particularly valuable for within-family 1 comparison of transgenic mouse aging pathways in terms of which aging gene regulatory pathways are common to spontaneous aging-like features in degus versus resistant in NMRs versus different transgenic mouse models. We identified common paths to longevity in NMRs, degus and humans.
